AFTER THE RI­OTS: RE­BUILD­ING THE COM­MUNITY: South L.A.'s Poverty Rate Worse Than '65

South Los Angeles - the epi­cen­ter of the deadly ri­ot­ing - lagged far be­hind the rest of the county dur­ing the 1980s in nearly every meas­ure of prosper­ity, and has a high­er poverty rate now for its fam­il­ies than it had in 1965, ac­cord­ing to new Census Bur­eau fig­ures. The stat­ist­ics un­der­score nu­mer­ic­ally the so­cial prob­lems that have plagued the area since the Watts ri­ots more than a quarter cen­tury ago: job­less­ness, hope­less­ness and a crip­pling lack of skills and edu­ca­tion.
